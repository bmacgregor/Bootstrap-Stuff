The Night Air

The door swings open - its hinges blessedly quiet despite the winter's frost that clings thickly to the metal. Sounds of men clearing some huge obstacle further along the road can be clearly heard, and from the sounds of the work, suggest they won't be finished for some time.

From the back of the wagon, Hedeg gropes blindly forward. 'The baggage train should be near the rear of the convoy,' he croaks. 'As for the hounds, they've been trained to obey the Church Knights well enough, but keep them hungry. The hound-master has their meat in his saddle-bags. If you can find some valerian leaves, you could mix it into their food. I'm sure it would do just the trick - valerian grows in the woods, and the hound master's bags are dark red, or so I'm told. Best be careful, though - the forest is most unkind to the unprepared...'

Searching the Camp

Now that everyone's in the relative light of the outdoors, the players can describe what they look like as they venture into a maze of troop supplies and irregularly placed wagons - a convoy far larger than the amount of Church Knights in the area would suggest; this must be part of the supply train for the Gray Church, and will most likely meet up with a significantly larger force in time, as any player with even a smattering of Soldiering will be able to tell.

With Hedeg's advice (and caution) the players are now free to explore as they like. Right now, they're unarmed, but the camp has a variety of things for the escapees to find. For the most part, the Church Knights are busy trying to move a giant tree that toppled across the road, nearly killing the lead horses and their riders, however, they will soon tire of trying, and given the late hour and the delay, command will issue a halt. Shortly after that, camp will begin to be set up, which will create a cat-and-mouse game for the players - unknown by the Church Knights - at least at first.

Ask the players what they're interested in doing - here are a few ideas:

  • Finding the Valerian (Survival): While it's true that Valerian does grow in the forest, it looks surprisingly close to Devil's Ire, a powerful stimulant that, when ingested, causes a flurry of activity. If taken in excess, the unfortunate victim goes into furious (and very noisy) spasms, generally followed by death. Although it'll definitely take care of the dogs in either case, using the second will definitely call the guards down around the players' ears. To notice the subtle differences between the Valerian and the Devil's Ire requires two (2) successes.

  • Finding the Saddlebag (Animal Ken): Finding the saddle-bag is just a matter of time for the players - the real feat is stealing the meat from a bag saddled to a trained horse. This will require two (2) successes to keep the horse quiet. Minor failure will cause the horse to neigh, which might be noticed, and leave the player without the dog's meals, while if no successes are gained, the horse will rear and call the attention of at least three or four guards.

  • Locating the Baggage Train (Soldiering): Based on Hedeg's directions, finding the baggage-train only requires a single (1) success. There are five dogs (four medium sized and one large) that stand a half-hearted watch here, however, if a player comes too close (within view), the hounds will immediately attack unless that player can calm them with a difficult Animal Ken check (3 successes). For the purposes of the timer (see below), making an Animal Ken check also counts as an action.

  • Stealing Supplies (Pick Pockets): Small arms can be found throughout the camp; anything dagger-sized requires one success pick pocket (1), with larger items requiring one (+1) more successes per range-increment. For example, because an Arming Sword has a reach of Medium (two steps past Hand - the range of a dagger), the Pick Pocket successes needed to find one lying around would be a three (3). Failure means the item's not available for easy thievery, and a complete botch will cause several guards to become alerted.

    Armour cannot be found in this manner, and will need to be claimed either from dead men, or from the baggage-train. Similarly, exotic weapons (a katana, for instance) are not typically carried by the Church Knights, and are therefore unavailable, however, should an enterprising player attempt to find a disguise, they can dress as a Church Knight by rolling one (1) Pick Pocket successes.

  • Finding out what Happened (Sneaking): Sneaking around to the front of the convoy to discover what caused the delay (and allowed the players fortuitous escape) is fairly easy at first, and later becomes more difficult. If the Knights haven't started to set up camp, it can be achieved with a single (1) success. However, if camp is beginning to be set up, a player will need two (2) successes to make their way to the front of the convoy (unless sneaking through the woods, which will require two (2) Light Step successes to cover their tracks in the snow), and three (3) successes if the guards have been alerted to their escape.

    Once at the front, they will find that several enormous trees were uprooted, and lay across the road in heaps. Clearing the damage will be impossible for the Church Knights, however, going around the wreckage should be possible (but difficult) once the sun is up. If the player is watching the Church Knights, they will also note when the order is given to set up camp, and will not need to roll a Soldiering check (see below).

A Word on Time

Of course, the players may elect to do a variety of other things (and most likely) will - apply the appropriate skill to the action, whatever it might be, and let them try what they will. Most checks will range from needing one success (easy) to at most three (journeyman) if the action would cause potential for discovery. Naturally, anything that would obviously call the guards down around the player's head's will do so, but for the most part, these actions are assumed to be done with an appropriate level of stealth in mind.

There is a timed element to all of the proceedings, however. Once half of the players have done something (found the Valerian, stolen a knife, etc.), the Church Knights will be told to set up camp for the night. Have all the players attempt Soldiering skill checks, requiring one (1) success. If achieved, the player notices something change in the camp atmosphere, and if two (2) successes are rolled, the player's military background signals to them that the order to halt for the day has been given. Naturally, this also calls for the prisoners to be fed, which is a problem for the escapees. This occurs whether or not the player was successful in their action - an attempt to do anything will count toward this timer ticking down. It goes without saying that, should a player be watching the Church Knights at the fallen trees, they automatically pass this check.

This realization might prompt one (or several) of the players to try and alert their fellows. They will now need to sneak through the camp in addition to any other skill check that they are attempting. Sneaking through the camp as the Church Knights begin to set up tents and prepare food requires one (1) Disguise success to maintain stealth. If the players have stolen a convincing outfit, they can attempt to blend in, so long as they keep their faces covered (the retinue is small enough that any Knight knows his brethren well, and an unfamiliar face will cause alarm).

Once each player has been able to act twice, the escape will be noticed by the camp cook, who will sound the alarm (if the players haven't been noticed already). In total, there are three-times the number of players in camp as guards, armed with various weapons. Two thirds of them are basic Rank-and-File grunts, while the last third are either Elites, the camp cook (a townsman), or the retinue's captain, who is uses the Champion stat-block. They should only be dangerous in large groups, or when the players are unarmed. Naturally, if this number seems too frugal, it can be increased.
