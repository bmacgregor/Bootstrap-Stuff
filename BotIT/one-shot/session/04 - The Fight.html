Discovered!

The players can get here a variety of different ways - being discovered early (perhaps by breaking stealth, alerting the dogs, or running out of time by dithering in the camp). Because of this you may have to treat this section a little differently than presented. It's written assuming your players have managed to stay hidden the entire time, and just discovered the Iron Cauldron at the end of Part Three - Getting Weapons, but because sessions can play out with huge differences, much of the text below should be taken with a grain of salt.

As you buckle on the last of your newly regained equipment, a shout goes up from deeper in the convoy. You can hear weapons being drawn and orders hastily given. It seems that the time for stealth is drawing to an end.

How the players choose to respond now is entirely up to them; here are a few options:

  • Maintain Stealth: the players may decide to remain hidden in the camp, using their relative stealth to their advantage against the Church Knights' greater numbers. A combination of sneaking and tracking can be used here to find isolated pockets of Knights and dispose of them, although fights lasting longer than a single round-per-combatant are likely to draw the attention of the rest of the Knights.

    Keep an eye on the number of Knights remaining, recalling that two-thirds of their number are basic Rank-and-File troops, and that of the last third, all but one are Elites, while the last - their captain - uses the Champion stat-block. Again, the total number of Church Knights is equal to three times the number of players.

  • Abandon Stealth: rather than stalking their foes throughout the camp, the players may elect to meet force with force. In this case, have them engage a small number of Church Knights at first, and gradually add to the number of enemies as the fight progresses.

  • Escape: escaping the convoy is detailed in the final section of the one-shot, which can be found here.
Of course, players being what they are (willful, insolent louts), these options are by no means an exhaustive list. They might try to draw the Knights off into the woods, setting traps and ambushes as they go, or some equally inventive way of harrying the convoy.

It should be noted that if the players venture too far into the forest, the denizens that live there are unlikely to allow them there for long. This is some distance away from the road that they're on (equivalent to a few-hours hike), but let them know that the forest is not a safe and friendly place. What resides there is beyond the scope of this adventure, but feel free to invent your own menaces.

Similarly, if the players delay for too long - several days - the convoy will be missed, and will be searched for by the Church. In this case, Witchfinders will come, expecting trouble and ready to bring grief in like kind. Although the Church Knights and the Witchfinders are both part of a greater whole in the Gray Church, they are totally dissimilar, with the Knights being a titular duty (they're not actually knights), held by the Rank-and-File, and the Witchfinders being a nasty bunch of zealots, completely devoid of mercy, but full to the brim with maddening hatred, low cunning, and paranoia.

As with the denizens of the forest, dealing with the Church's Witchfinders is beyond the scope of this one-shot, but as a ref, feel free to add them to your game as you see fit.

If the players decide to retreat to the forest, or after they kill the remaining Church Knights, the final section of the adventure can be found here.
