Into the Forest

If the players chose to escape or retreat:

You surge forward, away from the camp, and toward the tree-line its dubious freedom. Your newfound companions run with you, pounding their feet against the packed snow. Hedeg, the old blind man follows, groping across the frigid expanse. Within moments, you are plunging through the frosted undergrowth and into the forest. It's only then that you realize that, despite having escaped together, you don't know which side of the many faceted war any of your companions are on...

If the Players chose to fight:

The last of the Church Knights fall. Long moments pass, as you and your companions suck in huge gouts of air, breathing heavily after the fight. A heavy silence falls among you, and only now do you think to yourself that, despite having fought together, you don't know which side of the many faceted war any of your companions are actually on...

The End of the One-Shot
The Beginning of the Adventures

So ends the one-shot. There are a lot of items that have been left purposely vague, should your Players want to continue, here are a few things that got left out that you might (please read that with heavy italics - you should play your game the way your group wants to play, rather than sticking to anything written here) want to include, should you decide to stay in this campaign world:

  • Traem's Poisoners: Fornholm's old, dead King was poisoned, but not by his daughter, Alistra, as many believe. Instead, he was killed by Baelin Drannis, head of the Guild of Physikers, who is, in fact an Eldrani agent, and has been working with them for decades.

  • The Murder of Artorin: seeing a chance to seize the power vacuum created by his lord's death, Jerlix Fein - head of the Council of Lords - engineered the heir's grisly demise, and cast further suspicion on his sister, Alistra. In so doing, he has named himself Regent, and seeks further power, believing that he will also be also to stem the tide of the war with Eldran.

  • The Queen's Own: those staunch in their belief in Alistra's innocence count themselves among the Queen's Own. Although she has yet to be named Queen, her followers demand that the office be granted to her immediately, and have fought several skirmishes against the Lord's Guard. Their power-base tends to be set among the peasantry, though some nobles feel their lot might be better cast with Alistra, rather than the Lords.

  • Ranulf's War: although many see the civil war as the only item of import going on in Fornholm, this is certainly not the case. Despite being suggested by the Council of Lord's that 'Ranulf's War' in Eldran is a waste of resources, in fact, it's a desperate holding action, which, without proper support is doomed to fail, and will herald the Eldrin Empire's full might falling on the unprepared Fornholm.

  • The Iron Cauldron: a relic better left to the damned, the Iron Cauldron is a powerful artifact, capable of creating soulless warriors in exchange for the lives of the innocent. Steeped in the darkest necromancies of Lost Naivunnan, the Cauldron is a great evil, compounded by the fact that it is held by Mother Sarevin Caesless and her Gray Church. As with all things though, using the Cauldron has a cost.

  • Lost Naivunnan: a kingdom devoured by magic and evil, the Gray Church have unearthed several of Naivunnan's artifacts, and intend to use them to their advantage in the war for Fornholm. What they don't realize is that by using these tainted relics, they call Naivunnan back to the world, and awaken the dark that it vanished into a thousand years ago.
