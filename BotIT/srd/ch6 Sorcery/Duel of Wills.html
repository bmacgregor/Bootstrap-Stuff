The Duel of Wills

When two sorcerers battle, they don’t usually slug it out Mystery against Mystery (though they may of course), but engage in a Duel of Will instead. The Duel of Wills matches SP against SP in a way similar to how melee combat matches MP against MP, and it has a similar structure of Exchanges and Rounds.

First of all, a Duel of Wills requires two willing participants. It is not possible for one sorcerer to engage another in it against his will; thus, there is also no possibility of surprise. Each sorcerer uses an SP of his choice (usually his best) and announces this choice. There is no need for the SPs to derive from the same Mystery, and no need to stick to the same Mystery every Round. Penalties from Taint and other sources fully apply to the SPs during the Duel of Wills.

Duel of Wills Overview

One sorcerer is determined as initial aggressor and one as defender. In some cases, these roles might be obvious from the circumstances under which the Duel is entered, in other the participants might be in agreement about who is the initial aggressor. If neither is the case, the roles are assigned as desired by the sorcerer built with the better Sorcery Priority, or the higher SP if that is a tie, or the higher Power Attribute if this is a tie as well, or the higher Occultism Skill if tied again.

The aggressor then announces a sorcerous Offensive Maneuver and a number of dice from his SP assigned to it, to which the defender reacts by declaring a sorcerous Defensive Maneuver and also assigns dice. Both then pay any necessary Activation Costs for the chosen Maneuver from their SP, roll their assigned Maneuvers against their normal Casting TN for the Mystery used (6 to 8), applying any modifiers from the Maneuver chosen, in an Opposed Check.

If the aggressor is victorious, he retains initiative as aggressor, and there are additional effects as per the Maneuver chosen. If the Defender is victorious, he may assume the part of the aggressor and possibly also invoke additional effects as per his chosen Maneuver. In case of a tie, the aggressor simply retains this role.

If the first Exchange does not result in an immediate decision, a second Exchange follows, for which the remainder of the SPs can be used, exactly as per the melee rules. If this Exchange does still not yield a decision, the SPs refresh and a new Round of two Exchanges begins.

The eventual loser of the duel is thrown to the ground bodily under the onslaught of his opponent’s sorcerous power, where he lies stunned for the next Combat Round (all Pools at 0 dice). In addition, his sorcerous defences have been invaded and battered down by his opponent: For the remainder of the scene, he is at +2 TN to resist the victor’s sorceries and is at +2 Casting TN to affect the victor with his own sorcery. Finally, he acquires 1 point of Taint. Depending on the actual Offensive Maneuver by which he was overcome, the loser may also suffer physical damage on the Generic Damage Table. Armor Value never averts such injury, but BN soaks up some of it as normal.

While crippling injuries are entirely possible from a Duel of Wills, outright annihilation is rare. Wound Levels 1 through 4 are attained normally, by a result (Impact Rating – half BN) of 1 to 4 respectively. Wound Level 5 does however require a result of (Impact Rating – half BN) of 6+, and Wound Level 6 of 8+.

Under certain circumstances a group of sorcerers may gang up on a single sorcerer, pooling their ultramundane strength to best him. The circumstances under which this is possible and the mechanical aspects of combining efforts are exactly as with Combining Sorcerous Efforts, as are the consequences of defeat (ex. they are experienced by all to their full extent).

The Duel of Wills is then handled normally, with the leader of each group of combatants making all decisions and Checks. The only exception is that the Mystery used must remain the same throughout the contest. The default physical aspect of a Duel of Wills is the two sorcerers staring at each other intently, maybe breaking into sweat from the strain and swaying under the onslaught of the opponent’s will. For some settings it might however be appropriate to be a bit gaudier. Lightning flickering between the sorcerers, gusts of wind tearing at them, tongues of flame licking at them, vortexes full of barely glimpsed demonic entities wavering between them are only a small portion of the possibilities.

Lastly, Duel of Will may, at the ref’s discretion, be used between sorcerers and certain kinds of incorporeal creatures, whom the ref would have to assign the equivalent of an SP. It can also be used for battles between any hostile beings they encounter while using the Scrying Mystery, though the latter under these circumstances is at a decided TN advantage, being as they are on their home ground.

Sorcerous Maneuvers

All Maneuvers have an Activation Cost that has to be paid from the SP to utilize them. Some Maneuvers are easier to use with SPs based on certain Mysteries. Any such Mystery is listed as “Favoured” with the Maneuver. Activation Costs are 2 dice less if the SP used is based on a favoured Mystery, though never less than 0 dice, with the sole exception of the Endure defense, which may add +2 dice to SP if used with the Mending Mystery. Minimum Activation Costs are noted with the Maneuvers.

Offensive Maneuvers

Creep

Activation: 2 (minimum 0)

Favoured: Necromancy, Prophecy, Scrying

The attempt to find a weak spot in the opponent’s psychic defences and to get one’s assault through there. It is in effect a feint: The aggressor at first declares another Offensive Maneuver and only announces Creep once the defender has committed dice to a Defensive Maneuver. The aggressor may then add dice from his uncommitted SP to those dice already committed to the attack at the additional Activation Cost of 1 die per die added. The remainder of the offence is then resolved as if it was a Strike Maneuver.

Grind

Activation: 2 (minimum 0)

Favoured: Cursing

Grind is for keeping up a steady sorcerous assault to finally wear down the opponent’s defence. Assigned SP are checked against Casting TN and Opposed by enemy’s chosen defense. If Grind is victorious, half the QoS is added to the victor’s SP for the following Exchange, up to a maximum bonus dice equal to the number of dice in his SP at the beginning of the following Exchange. Grind is no means to win a Duel of Wills in itself; some other Offensive Maneuver is needed to finally deliver the decisive blow.

Overpower

Activation: 6 (minimum 4)

Favoured: Enslavement, Goety, Witchfire

The tactic of the sorcerer sure of his own superiority, this is a brutal onslaught of eldritch power, attempting to outright shatter the opponent’s defense. The assigned SP dice are checked against Casting TN and Opposed by the enemy’s assigned defence dice. If the defender wins or ties, he may immediately assume the part of aggressor.

If the aggressor is victorious, he has brutally broken through the opponent’s defences and inflicts damage of an Impact Rating equal to the total number of his Successes, not just those surpassing the defense Successes.

Strike

Activation: 0 (minimum 0)

The standard action of striking with one’s sorcerous power at the opponent. Check assigned dice against Casting TN, opposed by enemy’s chosen defense. If victorious, the victor may simply retain initiative as aggressor, or may alternatively choose to inflict damage of an Impact Rating equal to the QoS. If this Impact Rating fails to inflict at least a level 1 wound, the attacker overestimated the power of his strike: The Duel of Wills is still undecided and the previous defender assumes the role of the aggressor.

Defensive Maneuvers

Counterstroke

Activation: 3 (minimum 1)

Favoured: Cursing

The finicky and difficult tactic of turning the opponent’s power against himself. Defence dice are assigned and checked as normal, with a tie having the usual result of the aggressor retaining this role.

If the defender surpasses the attacker’s number of Successes, he immediately becomes the aggressor and attacks with a Strike on the very next Exchange (no choice here). He can assign as many dice from his SP as he wants to this Strike, and will have additional bonus dice equal to the previous’ aggressor’s number of Successes on the attack successfully defended against.

Deflect

Activation: 0 (minimum 0)

The standard tactic of simply deflecting the opponent’s sorcerous onslaught. If the number of Successes from the assigned SP checked against Casting TN equals the aggressor’s attack Successes, the attack was deflected. If it does not only equal, but actually exceed them, the defender may assume the mantle of aggressor.

Endure

Activation: 0 (minimum -2)

Favoured: Mending (including Pain), Necromancy

The tactic of hunkering down and taking everything the opponent throws at you in the hope that he will wear himself out. If the defence is successful, no matter to what degree, the defender may still not assume the role of aggressor. (See the sole exception below.) However, the aggressor for his next Exchange loses dice from his SP equal to half the number of Successes on his attack; the successful defender, who took the punishment, loses half as many dice as the aggressor, though always less than him (i.e. if the aggressor loses 1 die, the defender loses 0 dice).

If this results in the next Exchange starting with the current aggressor’s SP depleted and the current defender still retaining dice in his SP, the latter may assume the part of aggressor.

Evade

Activation: 0 (minimum 0)

Favoured: Prophecy

The tactic of getting the hell out of the fight, only possible if the sorcerer wanting to utilize it did not use an Offensive Maneuver in the immediately preceding Exchange; neither can this Maneuver be used on a confrontation’s very first Exchange. The assigned defence dice are checked against Casting TN –2, and if they at least tie the attack dice’s Successes, the Duel of Will is ended. It does however entail a certain degree of acknowledgement of defeat and inferiority.

For the remainder of the scene, the sorcerer ending a Duel of Wits by means of Evade is at Casting TN +1 to affect his opponent with any sorcery, and the opponent affects him at Casting TN –1.
