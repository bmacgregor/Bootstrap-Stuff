Health, Healing, and Wounds

In Blade, even a slight wound is no laughing matter. For the healing process to begin in earnest, there are some mandates:

First, you must be in a state of rest. Second, at least for the more serious wounds, you need some form of treatment. Wounds can be tended to by anyone possessing the Healing Skill. Note that each wound must be treated individually. A successful Healing Skill Check reduces Blood Loss by 3 per Success. A failed Healing Check increases Blood Loss by 1, while a Botched Healing Check doubles the wound’s BL.

Dealing with Bloodloss

As introduced in chapter four, at the very end of the Limelight in which the character first acquired BL, and on the end of each subsequent Limelight, he must check BN. This Check must yield Successes equal to or surpassing the current Blood Loss number.

With that in mind, any Limelight Blood Loss Check made while the subject is at rest (or at most, moving or walking very slowly), requires one less Success than indicated by the current Blood Loss rating to be successful. This includes characters unconscious from BL. However, even if a character’s Blood Loss rating is reduced to 0, the penalties accrued due to bleeding will not diminish immediately. Such penalties are reduced by 2 for MP, Archery Pool and SP for each passing day spent resting with proper nourishment and low activity, or by 1 per passing day of exertion.

Any Wound Level beyond 2 calls for a chirurgeon (someone with the Healing Skill at level 3 or higher). At the end of each week make a TY Check vs. the Pain rating of each individual wound. Each Success lowers that wound’s Pain value by 1. This Check is modified by three things: Rest, medical care, and damage type.

During convalescence, moderate activity with no more than one or two instances of strenuous activity is assumed. If a character spends a week completely at rest, with no more than occasional light activity, the TY Check for each injury is made against a TN of (Pain -1). On the other hand, if he doesn’t take it easy, this TN is (Pain +1).

A caregiver character using the Healing Skill and being available throughout much of the week of healing time may make a Skill Check before the patient rolls. Each Success in this Check lowers the difficulty of all patient healing Checks for this week by 1. Failure adds nothing, but if not a single Success is achieved on the Skill Check, all the patient’s healing Checks for the week are made at +2 TN.

Damage type — meaning Cleaving, Piercing, or Blunt— affect this TY Check as well. To heal a Blunt wound, the basic TN is equal to Pain, but Piercing or Cleaving injuries heal with a basic TN of Pain +1.

Falling Damage

Falling is defined as the free descent by force of gravity. All damage derived from falling is a result of both the distance fallen and the surface landed upon. See the following table.

Falling Damage Table

Landing Surface Damage Rating
Soft; a large pile of hay, a featherbed, water (with a successful Swimming check) One per Three Meters fallen
Mixed; a crowd of people, falling (not diving) into shallow water, muddy banks One per Two Meters fallen
Hard; ground, stone, armoured people falling from horses, water (with a failed Swimming check) One per Meter fallen

All damage caused by the fall is considered to be Blunt damage. Damage is randomly assigned to some location on the recipient’s body; for every two points of damage suffered, roll once on the following table and assign damage accordingly. Damage to any area rolled more than once is cumulative and applied with a single d6 roll on the Target Zone Charts (See Appendices).

Allocation of Falling Damage

d12 Roll Area Location
1 - 2 One
3 - 4 Two
5 Three
6 Four
7 - 8 Five
9 - 10 Six
11 - 12 Seven

Only the longest of falls can affect more than one Target Zone. The number of Zones the damage can be spread to can never be higher than two, or one quarter the fall’s height in meters, which ever is greater. As soon as this number of Zones has been derived from rolls on Table 5.4, further damage is assigned randomly to the Zones already determined.

Example: Otho is knocked from his horse. The fall from the saddle corresponds to a fall of 2m to the hard ground. Consulting Table 5.3, we see that’s a hard fall. He then rolls 3 and 4 on a D12. Consulting Table 5.4 we note that Otho takes a level two Blunt wound to his upper leg (area 2). He now must roll 1D6 on the Damage Table in the Appendices to determine the exact location of the injury.


Generic Damage

Any damage from fire, cold, electricity, sorcery, the elements, or not specifically covered by rules uses the “Generic Damage” chart in the Appendix. Such damage is calculated like damage from any other attack described in chapter four. Such damage is applied randomly using Table 5.4, unless impacting a specific area (such as someone with a flaming arrow, in which case any fire damage is simply applied to the area struck).

Poison

When a character is poisoned, he must pass frequent Checks to avoid advancing along the six levels of the Poison Track, which, much like the six Wound Levels of melee combat, are progressive in their severity. Should he advance to Poison Level 6, he dies. The frequency of such “Progression” Checks (using the Knockout Attribute) depends on how fast the poison acts. Such can either be:

  • A Limelight
  • A few minutes
  • Half an hour
  • A few hours

Ingested poisons generally have a progression rate of one half hour to several hours, unless sorcerous by nature, in which case they may have a progression time of only a few minutes.

Injected poisons generally have a progression rate of a few minutes to one half hour, but if injected to the head, neck or torso, become faster by one “step”. In such cases, a progression rate of but a single Limelight is possible. To successfully inject a poison, no Wound Levels need be inflicted. For as long as the poison attack’s Impact Rating surpasses the victim’s Armor Value, the poison is successfully injected, even if BN soaks up all actual damage.

To avoid advancing along the Poison Track, a Progression Check must be passed. This Check’s required amount of successes is dependent on the strength of the poison being resisted. Such Checks typically must yield either QoS 2 or 3. Poisons requiring QoS 1 certainly exist and are indeed much more common than the more virulent ones, but their progression is hardly dramatic enough to bother with at all.

Conversely, poisons necessitating QoS 4 or greater are possible, but so sublimely deadly that referees are strongly advised to have them appear more as a threat of something to avoid at all costs, rather than something to actually afflict on the PCs.

Medical aid can be provided to the afflicted by any character with either the Apothecary or the Healing Skill. A successful Apothecary Check with a Challenge Level equalling the virulence of the poison (i.e. the QoS required by the Knockout Check to avoid further progression on the Poison Track) will lower the TN of further Progression Checks by 1.

A Check using the Healing Skill brings the same benefit, but instead must meet a Challenge Level equal to twice the poison’s virulence. Once a character has passed three successive poison Progression Checks, further advancement on the Poison Track is arrested and the character has “ridden out” the poisoning. From this point on he must pass a Knockout Check every few hours at TN 7, with the poison recovery Challenge Level equal to the poison’s current virulence.

Successfully meeting this Check signifies one step back along the Poison Progression Chart (Table 25) and a commensurate reduction to the poison’s recovery Challenge Level. This downward spiral of Checks continues until the effects of the poisoning fade completely and the subject is hale once more.

Poison Progression Chart

Poison Level Effect Target Number
One Soreness and itching; slight nausea if ingested. Pain 1 9
Two Sweating and rapid breathing. Pain 3 8
Three Hyperventilating. Pain 5 8
Four Stops hyperventilating, but has trouble breathing. Pain 7, +1 Success required on all checks for any activity. Pass knockout Check (Challenge Level 1) to not pass out 7
Five Heart murmurs / palpitations. Pain 9, +2 Successes required on all Checks for any activity. Pass Knockout Check (Challenge Level 2) to not pass out 7
Six Coma followed by death within about half the progression time 6

Pain - It's Just a Scratch! (Optional)

The protagonists of Sword & Sorcery tales are notoriously tough. Whether because of an indomitable force of will or just sheer machismo, they often shrug off hurt that would lay low lesser men. The same is true for PCs and named NPCs in Blade.

As soon as the action in a scene in which the character has received a wound subsides, the higher of either BN or TY may be Checked for the character; (NOTE: PAs are not applied to this Check!). Once Checked, an amount of Pain equaling the QoS of the successful Check may be subtracted from any newly inflicted wounds. The player then decides how this QoS bonus is applied against those wounds, distributing Pain relief to any fresh wound as desired.

Example: Otho has 3 cuts received during the fight he just emerged from as victor:

  • A Pain 1 cut to his hand
  • A Pain 1 cut to his jaw
  • A Pain 2 cut to his side

Rolling a TN7 Check vs. Tenacity (His TY is higher than his BN) he scores 3 Successes and opts to reduce the Pain 2 cut to his side down to zero, and to also get rid of the Pain 1 cut to his hand. The cut to his jaw he opts to leave as a dueling scar for the ladies to admire!
