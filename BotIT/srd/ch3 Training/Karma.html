Karma

A special aspect of spending Passion Attribute points is Karma. One point of Karma is gained every time you spend a Passion point. These points come into play after your Character dies or leaves the game, allowing you to build a stronger Character.

When you are creating a new Character (whether the last one died or was simply “retired”), the Player may spend any number of his Karma points for better choices on the Talent Table, as shown on the following table.

Karma Rewards

Karma Bonus Priorities
11 - 30 E
31 - 50 D
51 - 70 C
71 - 90 B
91 - 110 A
111 - 130 A, C
131 - 150 A, B
151 - 170 A, A
171 - 190 A, A, C
191 - 210 A, A, B
211+ A, A, A

Higher Karma grants additional Priorities for Character creation. Thus, instead of one Priority A, B, C and so on, you might begin with one Priority A, two Priority B’s, one C, D and so on. Karma is earned and amassed by the Player, rather than the Character. Players can spend (or hoard) Karma across over several Characters. If the Character dies or retires permanently and the player creates another Character, even for a different campaign, he can spend part or all of his Karma to create a new and better Character. Karma that the player does not spend is retained by him, and noted on the appropriate space of the new Character’s sheet.

Karma is also earned by the Game Master, most likely in greater quantity than any other Player in his group. Every session, the GM receives Karma points equal to the Player who gained the most Karma during the session. These points act as a safeguard against the Game Master being left behind, should he one day become a Player himself. He can then use his amassed Karma to create a stronger character, just as his former Players did, who earned Karma while he was directing the game.
