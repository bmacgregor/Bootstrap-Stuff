Character Arc

A Character's Arc is central to who they are. It defines them as a person, informs their role-play decisions, and gives a good indication of how they might react to certain situations.

In a sense, it's linked to their background - how and where they grew up, the things they struggle with, what they want - but it also acts as a signal to the Game Master for the sorts of adventures that you, the Player, are interested in, as well as the expected outcome of those adventures.

Attributes

While a Character's Arc determines how they are role-played, attributes define what they're capable of, in-game. Every Character begins play with thirty points to spend on attributes, with the sole caution that only one attribute can be as low as 1, and only one can be as high as 8. Beyond this, Players can spend their points any way they like. Bonuses to each attribute are listed below.


Intellect

Intellect affects the power behind a Character's spells, with more intelligent Characters able to create powerful effects. Genius-level Mages are able to strip the flesh from their opponent's bones and warp reality with their minds.

For each point of Intellect above four, Characters add a +1 die bonus to their spell effects - both for damage as well as all other sorcerous abilities, making the casting much more impressive. For each point below four, they suffer a -1 modifier to all spell effects.

Brawn

Brawn modifies the amount of raw, physical damage a Duelist is capable of dishing out, with the mightiest of heroes able to cripple an opponent with a single strike; in combat, a Duelist with a focus on Brawn is a force to be reckoned with.

For each point of Brawn above four, Characters add a +1 die bonus to their physical damage scores, and add an extra die to bashes against inanimate objects for every other point. For Characters with Brwns scores of less than four, they suffer a -1 modifier to both physical damage rolls and attempts to bash inanimate objects.

Cunning

Cunning Duelists may lack power, but they make up for it in scope and speed, weaving spells that affect larger areas and able to deflect sorceries cast at them. Those with exceptionally high Cunning also benefit from faster casting times.

For each point of Cunning above four, Characters can expand their spells' radius by one step (as noted in the spell description). For every two points above four, they can cast spells one step faster (as indicated in the description) and add gain a bonus die for spell deflection rolls. For Characters with scores below four, their spell radii are reduced by one step, and they suffer a -1 penalty to all spell deflection rolls.

Agility

Duelists that focus on Agility are able to engage multiple opponents at once, and often use their speed in quick-draw style duels. They are better suited to dodging attacks and making follow-up ripostes, striking as soon as an opening appears.

For each point of Agility above four, Characters can engage one extra melee combatant at no penalty. For every two levels above four, they also gain a bonus die for all dodge rolls, and +1 speed bonus. For every point below four, a Character has great difficulty engaging multiple foes, and pays one extra point of stamina to defend against them. They also suffer a -1 penalty to speed and a -1 penalty to all dodge rolls for every two points of Cunning below four.

Will

Characters with a high will stat are able to hold and maintain spells, and build an impenitrable wall of defence. Will also governs how many spells can be cast before the Duelist needs to rest, and is a marker of a Character's sanity.

For each point of Will above four, the Character rolls an extra die for the purposes of sorcerous exhaustion. For every two points above four, a Character can maintain a spell one step longer, as outlined in the spell's description, and gains a an extra die to their mental shield. For every point below four, the Character takes a -1 penalty towards Checks for exhaustion. For every two points below four the Character suffers a -1 penalty to their mental shield rolls and their spell duration is shortened by one step.

Endurance

Duelists with high Endurance are capable of staying in fights the longest, as Endurance is directly linked to a Character's stamina. These workhorses have bonuses to their health, and can wear the heaviest armours and wield the largest weapons with ease.

For each point of Endurance above four, the Character rolls an extra die for the purposes of fatigue. For every two points above four, a Character can wear heavier armour and wield larger weapons at less of a penalty, as outlined in the equipment's description, and gains a bonus die to their physical defence. For every point below four, the Character takes a -1 penalty to fatigue Checks, and for every two points below four, are penalized for their equipment load, as outlined in the equipment's decription. They also take a -1 penalty to defence rolls.


A Word on Magic

Not every Player will want a magic capable Character, but half of the attributes in Duelist work with the magic system, and because of this there may be an inclination to dump those stats. First off, don't. Although magic is described later, there are overt cases - things like tossing fireballs, or walking through walls - however, there are a lot of covert and utility spells as well; subtle buffs, heightened senses, healing, and effects that have a much less flash to them.

In this way, Players can choose between creating a dedicated battle-mage, complete with world-shattering spells that lay waste to everything around them (a traditional 'blasty' wizard), or a talented warrior with preternatural abilities that, perhaps, they can't explain because they lack the flash of what most people think of as 'magical'.
